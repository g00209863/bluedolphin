Fish & Chips Arrive in Ireland

“The First Fish & Chips were sold in Dublin by Giuseppe and Palma Cervi who were Italian immigrants. They must have been a hardy couple because they landed in Queenstown(Cobh) and walked to Dublin.”

“The couple started selling fish & chips from a handcart which they pushed from pub to pub. The idea was well received by local people and they eventually found a permanent spot for their little business on Freat Brunswick Street (now Pearse Street).”

“Very Little is known about these 2 hard working people except that palma's grasp of the English language was not great and when a customer asked her for fish and chips she would point to the pile of chips and the tray of fish and say: "Uno di questa, uno di quella?" which meant "One of this, one of the other?"”

“Regualr customers soon copped on to Palma's way of thinking and this is how the Dublin expression of "One and One" came about when ordering fish & chips.
If you would like to know anymore about the history of Fish and Chips, talk to us - we have full of useless information!! HAHAHAH”

The Origin of Fish & Chips

“Fried fish is a Jewish dish which first appeared in London over 200 years ago. The recipe was brought over by Jewish immigrants who settled in the East End and the food was first mentioned in print by Charles Dickens in "OLIVER TWIST" which was published in 1838.”

“Legend says that chips were invented by a Belgium housewife who cut a few peeled potatoes into the shapes of small fishes and deep fried them for her hungry children. This recipe also made its way to London and once again was first mentioned by Charles Dickens in a "TALE OF 2 CITIES"(1859).”

“There were many outlets throughout England that sold fish or chips around this time but the first recorded case of anybody selling fish and chips was when a young Jewish fellow named Joseph Malin opened London's first fish and chips shop on Cleveland Street in 1860.”

“The first Fish & Chip restaurant was opened in 1896 by another Jewish immigrant called Samuel Isaacs and its popularity soon prompted him to open several more.
Fish & Chips have remained a top takeaway over the latter part of the 20th century and always will do providing that they are "Proper Fish & Chips cooked in the traditional manner.”
